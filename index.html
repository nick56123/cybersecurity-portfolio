Academics Beyond HCC

After earning my AA degree, I plan to transfer and complete my bachelor's degree at one of the nation's leading cybersecurity programs.

Transfer pathways from HCC — continuing toward a four-year cybersecurity degree

University of South Florida

B.S. in Cybersecurity (BSCyS)

The USF Cybersecurity program is accredited by ABET's Computing Accreditation Commission and is housed within the new Bellini College of Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Computing. Students build a strong technical foundation in programming, databases, networks, and statistics while also exploring the ethical, policy, and risk management dimensions of the field.

The program includes a mandatory capstone project, internship opportunities with Tampa Bay's growing tech sector, and access to cooperative education programs. Companies such as Lockheed Martin, ReliaQuest, and Rapid7 actively recruit from USF's talent pool. An accelerated BS-to-MS pathway also allows motivated students to earn a master's degree simultaneously.

Why I chose USF: As my primary transfer destination, USF offers ABET-accredited instruction, a thriving Tampa Bay tech community, and direct articulation agreements with HCC — making my transition seamless.

University of Central Florida

B.S. in Cybersecurity

UCF's Department of Computer Science offers a dedicated Bachelor of Science in Cybersecurity through its College of Engineering and Computer Science. The curriculum emphasizes hands-on skills in network defense, ethical hacking, cryptography, and digital forensics. UCF's Florida Center for Cybersecurity — known as Cyber Florida — serves as the state's central hub for cybersecurity research and education.

UCF boasts one of the largest cybersecurity research programs in the southeastern United States, giving students access to cutting-edge labs and research opportunities. The program also prepares students for industry certifications including CompTIA Security+, the Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), and CISSP.

Notable feature: UCF hosts major cybersecurity competitions such as the Florida Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition, offering students valuable real-world challenge experience.

Western Governors University

B.S. in Cybersecurity & Information Assurance

WGU's cybersecurity program is unique in its competency-based model, meaning students advance by demonstrating mastery rather than completing timed semesters. The curriculum is fully aligned with NICE (National Initiative for Cybersecurity Education) frameworks and includes six industry certifications bundled at no additional cost — including CompTIA A+, Network+, Security+, CySA+, PenTest+, and CASP+.

WGU is fully online, ABET-accredited, and recognized by the NSA and DHS as a National Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense Education. This makes it an excellent option for students who need a flexible schedule alongside employment or other commitments.

Notable feature: Graduates leave with six industry-recognized certifications embedded in their degree — a significant advantage when entering the job market.

Future Career

The cybersecurity field is projected to grow 29% through 2034 — nearly three times the national average. Below are three roles I am targeting in my career.

Security Operations Centers (SOCs) are the command hubs where cybersecurity professionals monitor, detect, and respond to threats in real time.
Information Security Analyst
ReliaQuest — Tampa, FL

As an Information Security Analyst, I would be responsible for monitoring an organization's networks and systems for security breaches and investigating violations when they occur. Day-to-day responsibilities include installing security software, documenting security incidents, and conducting simulated attacks to identify vulnerabilities before malicious actors can exploit them.

ReliaQuest is headquartered in Tampa, making it a prime local employer. Their GreyMatter platform uses AI-driven detection, and analysts work across a global security ecosystem protecting hundreds of enterprise clients.

Median: $124,910 / yr (BLS)
Penetration Tester
Rapid7 — Tampa, FL

A Penetration Tester — also known as an Ethical Hacker — is hired to legally simulate cyberattacks against an organization's systems, applications, and networks in order to find vulnerabilities before real attackers do. Using tools such as Metasploit, Burp Suite, and Nmap, penetration testers document their findings and recommend remediation strategies.

Rapid7 is a leading cybersecurity firm with a significant presence in Tampa. Working here would provide exposure to diverse client environments across finance, healthcare, and government sectors — helping me build broad offensive security expertise quickly.

Average: $143,000+ / yr
Cybersecurity Engineer
Lockheed Martin — Orlando / Tampa, FL

A Cybersecurity Engineer designs, implements, and maintains the security systems that protect an organization's networks and data. Responsibilities include building and managing firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and SIEM platforms. Engineers also develop security policies and procedures and respond to incidents when they arise.

Lockheed Martin is a major defense contractor that actively recruits from Florida universities. Working on government and defense contracts would provide exposure to classified systems and national security infrastructure — one of the most challenging and rewarding areas in the entire field.

Average: $130,000–$148,000 / yr

Emerging Technology

One of the most transformative technologies reshaping cybersecurity today is Artificial Intelligence — and it is the technology I am most excited to work with in my future career.

🤖

Artificial Intelligence in Cybersecurity

AI-powered security systems represent the next frontier in cyber defense — enabling organizations to detect, analyze, and respond to threats faster and more accurately than any human team could achieve alone.

A neural network model trained to classify network traffic — distinguishing safe connections from potential cyberattacks

How It Is Used

THREAT DETECTION AI systems analyze network traffic, system logs, and user behavior in real time to identify suspicious patterns — even novel threats that have never been seen before — by learning from millions of historical security events.
AUTO RESPONSE When a threat is detected, AI can automatically isolate affected systems, block malicious traffic, and trigger incident response playbooks — reducing response time from hours down to seconds.
PHISHING DETECTION Machine learning models scan emails for linguistic patterns, sender behavior, and contextual clues to flag phishing attempts before they reach end users — dramatically reducing the success rate of social engineering attacks.
BEHAVIORAL AI AI profiles normal user behavior and flags anomalies — such as logins from unusual locations or abnormal data transfers — that may signal compromised accounts or insider threats.

How I Will Use It & Why It Interests Me

MY ROLE As a future cybersecurity professional, I plan to work with AI-powered security platforms such as CrowdStrike Falcon and SentinelOne to monitor enterprise networks. Understanding how machine learning models are trained and deployed will be essential to my work.
EDUCATION FIT USF's new Bellini College of Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Computing directly combines both fields. My coursework will include AI and machine learning fundamentals alongside cybersecurity — giving me a rare dual expertise that the job market increasingly demands.
CAREER FIT The generative AI cybersecurity market is expected to grow nearly tenfold between 2024 and 2034. Professionals who understand both AI and security will be among the most sought-after in the industry — and that is exactly the intersection I am working toward.
WHY I CARE The same AI that defends us is being weaponized by attackers to craft smarter phishing campaigns, adaptive malware, and deepfakes. I find the ongoing battle between AI-powered attackers and AI-powered defenders to be one of the most intellectually stimulating challenges in technology today.
